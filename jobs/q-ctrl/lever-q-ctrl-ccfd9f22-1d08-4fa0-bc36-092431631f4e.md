About the team
The Sensing Engineering team at Q-CTRL transforms quantum research into deployable technologies that address real-world problems. We design, build, and field-validate an array of quantum sensors that support our key products, including Ironestone Opal - our solution for GPS-denied navigation.   
 
You don’t need a background in quantum to make an impact here. We value strong engineering fundamentals, a product mindset, and a drive to solve the hardest engineering problems in quantum sensing today.


About the role
We are seeking a Lead Integration Engineer to establish and lead the Integration Engineering team within our Sensing division, based in Huntsville, Alabama. This is an exciting opportunity to shape the integration function in the development of new quantum sensor prototypes and products, supporting our growing efforts to deliver software-ruggedized quantum gravimeters, accelerometers, and magnetometers for GPS-denied navigation applications.
As the Lead Integration Engineer, you will be responsible for the engineering strategy and execution of Q-CTRL’s quantum navigation products, ensuring that complex hardware and software subsystems function seamlessly together. You will define engineering standards, processes, and documentation to enable scalable development and ensure systems meet performance and reliability targets.
This is a hands-on role that combines technical leadership with team management, including planning, execution, oversight, and mentorship. You will work closely with engineers and scientists to integrate subsystems across electronics, optics, mechanics, and software, and support validation of complete systems in both laboratory and field environments. The ideal candidate is an experienced engineer with a strong background in relevant industries such as aerospace or other mission-critical fields, and a passion for building technology that delivers real-world impact.
