Planet designs, builds, and operates the largest constellation of imaging satellites in history. This constellation delivers an unprecedented dataset of empirical information via a revolutionary cloud-based platform to authoritative figures in commercial, environmental, and humanitarian sectors. We are both a space company and data company all rolled into one.

About the Role:

Our team is in charge of planning the tasks and imagery acquisitions of Earth observation satellites. We do so with a small team of highly-skilled engineers from our offices in SF and Berlin and we are currently looking for a highly motivated and proactive Satellite Tasking Engineer to join our operations team.

Your mission will be to support day-to-day operations, which includes collection performance analysis, order monitoring, performing feasibility studies and the development of our operational tools.&nbsp;

The ideal candidate is a self-starter, quick learner, good communicator, and has the ability to switch contexts quickly. You will be working in a close collaborative environment, and will gain first-hand experience in numerous fields related to managing satellite image acquisition planning, geospatial data analysis, tool development and process automation by supporting our operations team.&nbsp;

This is a full-time, hybrid role which will require you to work from our San Francisco office 3 days per week.

Impact You'll Own:

Support Collection Planning team during operational planning process

Prepare geospatial data, maps and dashboards

Run &amp; interpret standard and complex feasibility studies

Develops, validates and automates&nbsp; operational tools

Proactively improve existing processes

Collaborates with image quality support, orbits, mission software, space operations, data pipeline and product teams

What You Bring:

2+ years of relevant experience&nbsp;

Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, engineering, or geo science

Software development experience in Python or equivalent

Data-driven mindset and comfortable with data analysis

Sound SQL skills, familiar with git / version control

Understanding of satellite imagery and remote sensing

Self-starter that proactively tackles problems and brings in their own ideas

Ability to work in a dynamic environment

Team player with an ability to learn and collaborate across teams

Fluent in written and spoken English, the language of the company

What Makes You Stand Out:&nbsp;

Familiar with automation and software development tools (i.e. Jenkins, Bash, Git)

Experience with data visualization platforms (e.g., Looker Studio, OMNI, CARTO).

Familiarity with geospatial software (e.g., QGIS, ArcGIS, GDAL) or libraries (e.g., Shapely, GeoPandas).
