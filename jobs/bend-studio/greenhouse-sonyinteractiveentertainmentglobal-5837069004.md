Why Sony Interactive Entertainment?
Sony Interactive Entertainment isn’t just the Best Place to Play — it’s also the Best Place to Work. Sony Interactive Entertainment (SIE) is the company behind the PlayStation brand. As a subsidiary of Sony Group Corporation, we’re part of a proud legacy of innovation and excellence. SIE is a dynamic technology company, delivering cutting-edge hardware and network services to more than 100 million people and an entertainment leader, home to some of the most beloved and recognizable intellectual properties (IP) in the world. Our role at SIE is to create and nurture the experiences under the PlayStation brand, a name synonymous with entertainment excellence and creativity.
About the Advanced Technology Group&nbsp;
The Advanced Technology Group (ATG) is a part of Foundational Systems and Experiences Engineering (FSEE) within the Platform Business Group.&nbsp;Established over 20 years ago as part of&nbsp;PlayStation®’s&nbsp;first-party game development studios, ATG has always focused on helping game developers get the most out of PlayStation®&nbsp;hardware&nbsp;-&nbsp;a mission that continues to this day.&nbsp;As a team, we&nbsp;are responsible&nbsp;for&nbsp;large parts&nbsp;of the&nbsp;graphics&nbsp;software stack used by all PlayStation®&nbsp;games,&nbsp;and&nbsp;collaborate with&nbsp;leading&nbsp;engineering teams worldwide, from game developers to hardware vendors, to deliver key platform technologies.&nbsp;
What&nbsp;you’ll&nbsp;be doing&nbsp;
You will be working on Razor GPU, a GPU performance analysis and debugging tool, for current and future PlayStation®&nbsp;platforms.&nbsp;
You will be developing GPU Capture and Replay technology, which allows GPU&nbsp;commands, shaders and resources to be captured,&nbsp;analysed and later&nbsp;reinstated,&nbsp;so that&nbsp;graphics&nbsp;frames&nbsp;can be re-executed on the GPU independently from the running game.&nbsp;
You will be developing&nbsp;profiling and debugging features upon this core technology, to help game developers worldwide to realize their creative vision and push PlayStation®&nbsp;hardware to its limits.&nbsp;
You will be developing&nbsp;internal&nbsp;features&nbsp;used by&nbsp;platform software&nbsp;and hardware&nbsp;teams,&nbsp;to help build and improve the&nbsp;PlayStation®&nbsp;graphics software stack, as well as diagnose low-level&nbsp;platform&nbsp;issues.&nbsp;
You will collaborate with game developers and platform engineers to&nbsp;gain a deep&nbsp;understanding of&nbsp;their&nbsp;needs, with a view to creating efficient,&nbsp;reliable&nbsp;and scalable tools, which are a joy to use&nbsp;for&nbsp;entry/new-level&nbsp;and deeply technical engineers alike.&nbsp;
This is a unique senior&nbsp;engineering role&nbsp;at the&nbsp;heart&nbsp;of PlayStation®&nbsp;GPU technology, where you will work within a highly experienced&nbsp;technologytechnical-driven team. We are looking for&nbsp;developers&nbsp;who are passionate&nbsp;about their craft, the products they&nbsp;build, and who would like to make a&nbsp;lasting&nbsp;impact on the&nbsp;PlayStation®&nbsp;graphics software stack,&nbsp;its tooling,&nbsp;and&nbsp;future.&nbsp;
&nbsp;What&nbsp;we are looking for&nbsp;
Strong problem-solving skills, including the ability to design and implement&nbsp;highly reliable&nbsp;performant&nbsp;systems in C++.&nbsp;
Strong debugging skills, including the ability to understand&nbsp;and reason about&nbsp;complex systems,&nbsp;as well as&nbsp;unpick their interactions.&nbsp;
Experience developing&nbsp;on&nbsp;consoles,&nbsp;particularly&nbsp;using&nbsp;console&nbsp;graphics and memory management APIs.&nbsp;
An interest in&nbsp;GPU architecture&nbsp;and&nbsp;low-level graphics&nbsp;engineering.&nbsp;
Experience&nbsp;using&nbsp;GPU profiling and debugging tools.&nbsp;
An&nbsp;eagerness&nbsp;to learn&nbsp;and take responsibility&nbsp;of&nbsp;features end-to-end, from&nbsp;building&nbsp;core&nbsp;systems,&nbsp;to&nbsp;influencing&nbsp;end-user&nbsp;UX&nbsp;design.&nbsp;
